The parable of the two young fish

Posted on Aug 30, 2018 by Klaus Paul 2 mins read

Some time ago I stumbled upon this commencement address where I’ve found this fascinating parable of the two young fish:

…There are these two young fish swimming along and they happen to meet an older fish swimming the other way, who nods at them and says “Morning, boys. How’s the water?” And the two young fish swim on for a bit, and then eventually one of them looks over at the other and goes “What the hell is water?”…

The moral of the story: “…The most obvious, important realities are often the ones that are hardest to see and talk about…Stated as an English sentence, of course, this is just a banal platitude, but the fact is that in the day to day trenches of adult existence, banal platitudes can have a life or death importance”.

To me, business analysts are the older fish, aware of the realities of the enterprise, as a result of our analysis work. The younger fish are the ones turning a blind eye to the harsh facts (the important realities) exposed through our work.

However, instead of swimming away from the young fish, I am of the opinion it is our duty to help them realize they are in this thing called water, and bring them away from the flatness we, from time to time, found ourselves on.
