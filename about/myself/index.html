About me

Posted on Oct 02, 2019 by Klaus Paul 2 mins read

What do I like about being a BA?

Is the chance to apply analytical and problem-solving skills towards problems/opportunities within a company; is being the bridge between business and IT (in software development projects) and even between different business units! Is ensuring the right solution is found and delivered.

What don’t I like about being a BA?

Is the perception that most people still have that a BA is a note taker, a simple creator of documentation that is only required by the process, but never really used by anyone else. Also, the fact that most projects bring the BA in after he/she was most needed.

What do I bring to the table?

I bring strong analytical and problem-solving skills, coupled with a long experience in different industries (internet, manufacturing, financial services, printing, metallurgic, and banks), roles (database analyst, software tester, system analyst, and business analyst), and project methodologies (Agile and Waterfall), focused on results. I have also been told that I have a talent to analyze, synthesize, and explain complex problems.
